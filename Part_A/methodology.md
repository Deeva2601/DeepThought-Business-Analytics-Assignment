# Methodology: Target Company Research (Ahmedabad-Vadodara Hub)

## 1. Executive Summary
This document outlines the methodology used to identify, profile, and score 25 "Federer" specialty manufacturers in the Ahmedabad-Vadodara industrial belt of Gujarat, India. The target segment was restricted to companies with a revenue range of **Rs. 50 Cr – Rs. 500 Cr**, focusing on technocrat-led entities with a demonstrable technical moat.

## 2. Hub Selection: The "Federer" Belt (Ahmedabad-Vadodara)
The Ahmedabad-Vadodara industrial corridor (including Ankleshwar and Nandesari) was selected as the research hub for the following reasons:
- **Concentration of Specialty Chemicals:** This region contributes over 60% of India's specialty chemical output.
- **Technocrat Ecosystem:** Many companies here were founded by alumni of Maharaja Sayajirao University (MSU) or technical professionals who returned from the USA/Germany.
- **Infrastructure:** The GIDC (Gujarat Industrial Development Corporation) clusters in Vatva, Naroda, and Makarpura provide a structured ecosystem for mid-sized manufacturers.

## 3. Sourcing Strategy
The identification process followed a multi-stage filtering funnel:
1.  **Top-Down Screening:** Utilized B2B directories (IndiaMART, TradeIndia) and industry associations (Vatva Industries Association, Federation of Gujarat Industries) to identify active manufacturing units.
2.  **Financial Filtering:** Cross-referenced candidates with financial databases (**Tofler**, **Tracxn**, and **Screener.in**) to isolate companies in the Rs. 50Cr - 500Cr revenue bracket.
3.  **Qualitative Profiling:** Investigated LinkedIn profiles and company "About Us" pages to verify if the Managing Director or Founder has a technical background (PhD, M.Sc, or Engineering).
4.  **Growth Signal Analysis:** Looked for recent news regarding IPOs (e.g., Paragon Fine), new plant inaugurations (e.g., Chemcon P10/P11), or capacity expansions (e.g., AksharChem Silica plant).

## 4. Scoring Logic (C1-C6)
Each company was evaluated against six core criteria:
- **C1 (Manufacturer):** Does the company have in-house manufacturing? (Traders/CROs disqualified).
- **C2 (India-based):** Is it an Indian promoter-led entity? (MNC subsidiaries disqualified).
- **C3 (Technical Moat):** Does it produce differentiated products like SDAs, niche enzymes, or complex intermediates?
- **C4 (Technical DM):** Is the Managing Director a technocrat?
- **C5 (Growing Sector):** Is the industry (Specialty Chemicals/Biotech) in a tailwind phase?
- **C6 (Growth Signals):** Are there recent indicators of scale (CAPEX, revenue CAGR)?

## 5. Verification & Guardrails against Hallucination
To ensure data accuracy and counter AI hallucination:
- **Revenue Check:** Every revenue figure was cross-checked against public market cap data (for listed firms) or Registrar of Companies (ROC) filing summaries via Tofler/Zauba.
- **Location Check:** Verified that manufacturing units are physically located in GIDC clusters within the selected hub.
- **Disqualification Rules:** Explicitly removed companies like **Aarti Industries** or **Meghmani** which have breached the Rs. 1000Cr+ revenue mark, as they no longer fit the "mid-sized" target.

## 6. Final Selection
The final 25 companies represent a diverse mix of:
- **Specialty Chemicals:** 16 Companies (Custom synthesis, pigments, intermediates).
- **Biotechnology/Pharma:** 7 Companies (Enzymes, vaccines, APIs).
- **Niche Engineering/Minerals:** 2 Companies (Perlite, Filtration).

These companies exhibit high "Federer" potential—technical excellence, independent management, and strong growth momentum.
